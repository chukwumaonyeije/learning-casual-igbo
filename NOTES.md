# Teaching Preferences

- Keep lessons short: usually 5–10 minutes.
- Use spaced repetition and retrieval practice, not passive rereading.
- Prefer practical reading to long grammatical lectures.
- Teach practical, casual spoken Igbo first when the lesson is conversational; grammar serves understanding and speech.
- Include pronunciation help and listening-style shadow practice in text form.
- Use culturally meaningful examples where appropriate: Igbo names, family/community language, Scripture, proverbs, and everyday phrases.
- Include family, church, village, travel, food, and everyday Nigerian social settings.
- Prefer natural phrases over textbook phrasing; build confidence speaking before emphasizing writing in the conversation track.
- Build from letters → syllables → words → sentences → short paragraphs.
- Use standard Igbo unless dialect comparison is explicitly requested.
- Mark a translation or pronunciation as uncertain when it has not been verified.

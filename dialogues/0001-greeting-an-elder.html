DIALOGUE 0001 · ELDER

A warm, respectful greeting

IgboMeaningReading help
You: Ndewo, Mama.Hello, Ma’am/Mama.Keep Ndewo smooth; do not split it into English letters.
Elder: Ndewo.Hello.Reply can be the same greeting.
You: Kedu ka ị mere?How are you?Read the dotted ị as its own vowel.
Elder: Adị m mma.I am fine.Notice ị again.
You: Daalụ.Thank you.Two clear vowel beats: daa-lụ.

Cultural note

Use the title your family uses. In a real setting, follow the elder’s lead and a relative’s local practice.

Short variation

Ndewo, Papa. — Hello, Sir/Papa.

Practice prompt

Cover the table. Say the first and third lines to an imagined elder. Then substitute the family title you would genuinely use.

Standard-Igbo starter dialogue; local phrase choices may vary.

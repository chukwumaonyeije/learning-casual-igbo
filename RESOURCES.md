## Dictionaries and lexical tools

- [Igbo Dictionary](https://www.igbodictionary.com/) — searchable community dictionary; useful for checking words and audio where available. Verify individual entries against a second source.
- [Igbo API](https://igboapi.com/) — open dictionary/language-data project; useful as a technical reference and for word lookup. Verify learner-facing glosses and tones.

## Conversational listening and practice

- [YouTube search: Igbo greetings](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Igbo+greetings+standard+Igbo) — use only channels that identify fluent speakers and context; compare greetings with trusted family/community usage.
- [Omniglot — Igbo](https://www.omniglot.com/writing/igbo.htm) — introductory orthography overview. Useful for orientation, not a primary authority for spoken usage.

## Scripture for graduated reading

- [Bible.com — Igbo translations](https://www.bible.com/languages/ibo) — locate a familiar passage in Igbo alongside a known-language translation. Confirm the translation and orthography shown before using it for a lesson.
- [Bible.is](https://www.bible.is/) — another platform on which Igbo Bible audio/text may be available. Confirm availability and translation label.

## Academic and library discovery

- [University of Wisconsin–Madison African Languages resources](https://africa.wisc.edu/african-languages/) — a sensible university starting point for locating African-language programs and materials.
- [Endangered Languages Archive (ELAR)](https://www.elararchive.org/) — research archive; search for Igbo collections and use only material whose access conditions permit it.
- [Google Scholar: Igbo orthography](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Igbo+orthography) — use to locate academic treatments of spelling and tone, ideally through a library.

## How this workspace will use sources

1. Use dictionaries to verify a word's spelling, meaning, and—when supplied—tone/audio.
2. Use Bible text only in short, contextual excerpts with translation attribution.
3. Cross-check a new form before putting it into a permanent reference card.
4. Record source links in the relevant lesson or learning record as the course grows.
5. For casual phrases, ask a trusted family or community speaker whether a form sounds natural in their setting; record regional variation rather than treating one household's usage as universal.

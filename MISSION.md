# Igbo Reading & Conversation Workspace

## Goal

Build confident, practical reading and casual conversation in standard Igbo: from recognizing letters to reading meaningful passages aloud, and from greetings to short, natural exchanges with family and community.

## Why this matters

This is a heritage-language project. Reading and speaking create a dependable bridge to names, family and community language, Scripture, proverbs, and everyday Igbo—even on days when conversation practice is not possible.

## What success looks like

I can read simple Igbo words and sentences aloud; identify the major letters and sounds; recognize dotted vowels and tone marks; greet people naturally and respectfully; introduce myself; ask simple questions; understand common responses; and participate in short casual exchanges.

## First milestone

Complete the first reading and greeting lessons, accurately read familiar words containing all eight vowels and common digraphs, and complete a short greeting exchange with both a peer and an elder.

## Working rhythm

Aim for 5–10 minutes daily. Each session should include quick recall of older material before one new, focused reading or conversational skill.

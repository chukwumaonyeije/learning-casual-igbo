IGBO READING · LESSON 0001

5–10 MINUTES

The alphabet is your map

Today’s skill: notice the units that make Igbo spelling readable—especially dotted vowels and digraphs.

One essential idea

Standard Igbo has 36 alphabet units. Some units use two characters but are read together: ch, gb, gh, gw, kp, kw, nw, ny, sh. Three vowels carry a dot: ị, ọ, ụ. That dot belongs to the letter.

Unlike English, resist guessing from an English spelling habit. Let the written Igbo units lead your eyes.

Your first scan

Look forWhat to doExample
Dotted vowelKeep it distinct: ị / ọ / ụụlọ
DigraphRead it as one unitakwụkwọ → kw
Tone markNotice it when it is printedá / à

Tone marks matter, but are often left out in everyday print. We will grow into them; today, simply notice them.

Practice words

Say each slowly. First point to the special unit; then read the whole word.

ụlọ — house

Find: ụ

akwụkwọ — book

Find: kw and ụ

anyị — we / us

Find: ny

Read aloud

Read these once slowly, then once smoothly. You are training your eyes—not auditioning for perfection.

ụlọ · akwụkwọ · anyị · nne · ezi

Quick check

1. Which vowel is dotted?

2. In akwụkwọ, which is a single reading unit?

3. What should you do when you see ny?

Retrieval practice: look away

Before revealing the answer, say aloud: Which three vowels are dotted? Name two digraphs. What is the special unit in anyị?

Answers: ị, ọ, ụ; any two of ch, gb, gh, gw, kp, kw, nw, ny, sh; and ny.

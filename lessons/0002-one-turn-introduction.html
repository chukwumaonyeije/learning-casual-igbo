CASUAL IGBO · LESSON 0002

5–10 MINUTES

Say your name

Today’s skill: make a tiny introduction after a greeting—then ask the other person’s name.

Warm-up: retrieve first

Before continuing, answer aloud: How would you greet an elder? How would you say “I am fine”? Then do the greeting-recall drill once without peeking.

The useful pair

IgboMeaningReading cue
Aha m bụ Chukwuma.My name is Chukwuma.Aha m bụ + your name. Keep the dotted ụ.
Aha gị bụ gịnị?What is your name?Notice gị and gịnị: each has dotted ị.

You do not need a grammar lecture to use these. Treat each as a reusable conversational shape.

Put it after a greeting

You: Ndewo.

You: Aha m bụ Chukwuma.

Other person: Ndewo. Aha m bụ Ada.

You: Aha gị bụ gịnị?

In a real exchange, do not mechanically repeat both name lines. Use the one that matches what you need to say; this is a practice scaffold.

Make it yours

Say this aloud three times, replacing the name with your own:

Aha m bụ [your name].

Then say the question once, without reading: Aha gị bụ gịnị?

Quick check

1. Which phrase means “My name is Chukwuma”?

2. Which word contains the dotted vowel in “What is your name?”

Retrieval practice: look away

Say your greeting, your name, and “What is your name?” without looking. Now imagine an elder: begin with Ndewo and the family-appropriate title before your introduction.

Ndewo, Mama. Aha m bụ Chukwuma. Aha gị bụ gịnị?

Use the title and exact greeting your family prefers.

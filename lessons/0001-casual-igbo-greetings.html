CASUAL IGBO · LESSON 0001

5–10 MINUTES

Start with warmth

Today’s skill: greet a friend and an elder in a simple, socially aware way.

Two lanes, one principle

With a friend, a brief Kedu? can open a warm check-in. With an elder, choose a respectful greeting such as Ndewo, Mama. The title should match your family’s practice.

SituationSayMeaning
FriendKedu?How are you? / How is it?
ElderNdewo, Mama.Hello, Ma’am/Mama.
ReplyAdị m mma.I am fine.
ThanksDaalụ.Thank you.

Say it with your eyes on the spelling

Ndewo: say it as one smooth word. Daalụ: take two clear beats, daa-lụ. In Adị m mma, notice the dotted ị. Do not use English spelling guesses; practise alongside a fluent person or verified audio when possible.

Mini-dialogue

You: Ndewo, Mama.

Elder: Ndewo.

You: Kedu ka ị mere?

Elder: Adị m mma.

You: Daalụ.

Good social instinct: follow your own family’s titles and greeting pattern when they differ.

Quick check

1. You are greeting an elder you call Mama. What is the strongest starter?

2. What is a simple answer to Kedu??

Look away, then retrieve

Without looking, say: one greeting for a friend; one greeting for an elder; “I am fine”; and “thank you.”

Kedu?; Ndewo, Mama/Papa.; Adị m mma.; Daalụ.

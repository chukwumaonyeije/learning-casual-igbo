## Intended learning

Use a small, natural greeting set in two social contexts: a casual peer check-in and a respectful greeting to an elder. The learner should prioritise their own family’s preferred titles and variants.

## Phrases introduced

- `Ndewo` — hello / respectful greeting
- `Ndewo nu` — hello, everyone
- `Kedu?` — how are you? / how is it?
- `Kedu ka ị mere?` — how are you? (fuller check-in)
- `Adị m mma.` — I am fine.
- `Daalụ.` — thank you.
- `Ụtụtụ ọma` — good morning

## After the lesson, I should be able to

- Greet a friend with `Kedu?` and respond `Adị m mma.`
- Greet an elder with `Ndewo` plus an appropriate family title.
- Say `Daalụ` naturally after an exchange.
- Name one phrase that varies in my family or dialect community.

## Intended learning

Recognize that standard Igbo has 36 alphabet units, including eight vowels; spot the dotted vowels `ị`, `ọ`, and `ụ`; and treat combinations such as `ch`, `gb`, `gw`, `kp`, `kw`, `nw`, `ny`, and `sh` as single reading units.

## After the lesson, I should be able to

- Point out the dotted vowels in a word.
- Avoid reading `ch` and `sh` as two separate letters.
- Read several short practice words slowly, left to right, by their Igbo spelling.
- Explain that a visible tone mark matters, while many everyday texts leave tone unmarked.

## Review next time

Without looking: name the eight vowels, identify the dotted-vowel trio, and read `ụlọ`, `akwụkwọ`, and `anyị`. Then introduce syllable chunking and a few high-frequency words.

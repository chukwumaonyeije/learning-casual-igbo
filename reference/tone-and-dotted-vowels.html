Two things to notice

1. Dotted vowels change the letter

iplain vowel
ịdotted vowel
oplain vowel
ọdotted vowel
uplain vowel
ụdotted vowel

Keep the dot visible when copying a word. It can distinguish one vowel from another.

2. Tone can be shown over vowels

Beginning convention: á marks high tone, à marks low tone, and an unmarked vowel often represents mid tone in teaching notation. In everyday Igbo print, tone marks are often omitted unless needed for clarity.

For now: read every tone mark you see; do not guess a missing mark. Build listening practice alongside reading.

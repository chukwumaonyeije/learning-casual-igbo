Greeting elders with care

In Igbo social settings, greeting is relationship work. Lead with warmth; let local family practice guide you.

MoveExampleWhy it helps
Use a respectful greetingNdewo, Mama.Clear, warm opening.
Use an appropriate titleNdewo, Papa.Names/titles vary by family; follow local preference.
Ask after wellbeingKedu ka ị mere?A polite fuller check-in; literal word-for-word analysis can wait.
Answer simplyAdị m mma. Daalụ.“I am fine. Thank you.”
Cultural note: greeting customs and titles vary across families and dialect areas. When unsure, mirror the greeting and title used by a trusted relative; that is socially wiser than performing a memorized script.
Do not overgeneralize a household practice into “all Igbo people say…”

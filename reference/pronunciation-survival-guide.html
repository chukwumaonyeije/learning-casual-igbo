Pronunciation survival guide

Text can guide your eyes; fluent voices train your ear. Aim for clear units, not English approximations.

NoticePractical move
Dotted vowels ị, ọ, ụThey are distinct vowels. Do not erase the dots when copying or reading.
ch, sh, ny, kw and other digraphsRead each as one unit; pause briefly before it if it is new.
Written tone marksNotice á and à when supplied. Everyday writing may omit them.
Practice methodLook → say slowly → say smoothly → repeat after a fluent recording or person.
For today: say Ndewo in one smooth shape, then practise Ndewo, Mama. Do not force an English “w” or swallow the final vowel.
